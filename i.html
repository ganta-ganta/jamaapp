The Farmer's Struggle

For many farmers in India, the cycle of debt begins even before the crop is sown. Ramu, a small farmer in Andhra Pradesh, borrows money at high interest rates every season just to buy seeds and fertilizers.

He struggles to decide which crop or seed variety to choose. Once the crops are in the ground, unpredictable weather, pests, and volatile markets put his family's livelihood at risk. One bad season is enough to trap him in debt.

Our Solution - Jama App

Jama App is a humble attempt to make farming knowledge — weather, prices, crop science, and government schemes — accessible to every farmer in their own language.

Aided by visuals and audio to bridge education gaps, our app empowers farmers with information that was previously difficult to access.

About UruVada Innovations

We, Mohan Kantamaneni and Rama Krishna Ganta, come from farming families in a small village in Andhra Pradesh. Growing up, we saw firsthand how uncertainty defined agriculture.

These experiences shaped our belief that farming must change if our villages, and ultimately our country, are to progress. With this in mind, we founded UruVada Innovations.

Uru meaning village or town, and Vada meaning street or neighborhood. Our mission is to empower farmers with technology that's accessible and easy to use.
